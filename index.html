2022-11-23 Winter is worse

In recent years, I’ve started to like winter more and more. It’s cozy (though unfortunately, there is hardly any snow to be seen in the Netherlands) and I mind the cold less than I used to (perhaps also just a side effect of living in a slightly warmer place).

However, winter seems to have the negative effect that all my friends go into social isolation mode. They’re pretty much all exclusively introverted and I really need to take initiative for anything at all to happen.

And I’m tired of doing that all the time. There are perhaps two people who occasionally make the first step and ask for a shared lunch or something like that, but for everyone else—if I let them—it’s permanent radio silence. If I had more friends or more to do in general, I would probably just ditch anyone who’s not willing to put in the slightest amount of effort to keep up a friendship.

Also, on a complete aside, dating apps really are an utter waste of time and energy (see also ‘Why am I talking to 10 guys?’ The rise and fall of dating apps, by Annie Lord). Every single of the very few recent matches (for the span of about half a year) has either immediately unmatched me or stopped writing after the first message.

2022-08-22 Lille

I spent the weekend with friends in Lille (France), a wonderful, small town that I greatly enjoyed. I would like to live in a place like it. Unfortunately, my understanding of French is at best rudimentary and in order to get by there (even as a visitor), you need to speak the language. And for that, I don’t think I have the drive right now.

One thing that caught me off-guard quite a bit was how much it emotionally impacted me to travel with two close couples. As time went by, I found it increasingly difficult to not feel miserable about me in my perpetual single existence. I guess I didn’t think of this because when we hang out, usually my friends are not accompanied by their partners.

2022-08-18 Thoughts on dating apps

I’ve tried Bumble, Tinder, Happn, and Hinge in the last year and they just don’t do it for me. I went on three dates. They were fine. In general I hardly get any likes or matches. The net effect up until now is that my self perception took a dent and I’m none the happier. For what it’s worth, Bumble and Hinge are pretty nice in terms of their product.

On limited likes

All dating apps I’ve tried severely limit the amount of people that I can like per arbitrary time interval (usually 24 hours). This is trying to make you buy whatever pay-to-date features they have on offer. This isn’t a convincing sales conversation for me because after so much time spent running into these limits, I can’t imagine how 2 likes per month instead of 1 would make the difference.
